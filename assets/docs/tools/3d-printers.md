# 3D Printers

Our Fablab has several 3D printers for creating plastic prototypes and finished products.

## Available Printers

### Prusa i3 MK3S+
- **Print Volume**: 250×210×210mm
- **Materials**: PLA, PETG, ABS, TPU
- **Layer Height**: 0.05-0.35mm
- **Best for**: High-quality prototypes, functional parts

### Ultimaker S3
- **Print Volume**: 230×190×200mm
- **Materials**: PLA, ABS, PETG, TPU, Nylon
- **Layer Height**: 0.06-0.2mm
- **Best for**: Professional-quality prints, dual-material printing

### Formlabs Form 3
- **Print Volume**: 145×145×185mm
- **Materials**: Various resins (Standard, Tough, Flexible)
- **Layer Height**: 0.025-0.1mm
- **Best for**: High-detail miniatures, jewelry, dental models

## Getting Started

1. **Design your model** using CAD software like Fusion 360 or Tinkercad
2. **Export as STL** file format
3. **Slice your model** using appropriate software:
   - PrusaSlicer for Prusa printers
   - Cura for Ultimaker
   - PreForm for Formlabs
4. **Reserve printer time** through our booking system
5. **Print your object** with supervision

## Print Settings Guidelines

### Layer Height
- **0.1mm**: High detail, slow printing
- **0.2mm**: Good balance of quality and speed
- **0.3mm**: Fast printing, lower detail

### Infill Density
- **10-15%**: Lightweight prototypes
- **20-30%**: Standard functional parts
- **50%+**: High-strength applications

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues
- **First layer not sticking**: Check bed leveling and temperature
- **Stringing**: Reduce temperature or increase retraction
- **Layer separation**: Increase temperature or check for drafts
- **Warping**: Use heated bed and enclosure for ABS

Need help? Ask a staff member or check our [troubleshooting wiki](/docs/tutorials/3d-printing-troubleshooting/).
