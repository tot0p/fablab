# Laser Cutters

Our laser cutters can cut and engrave a wide variety of materials with precision and speed.

## Available Machines

### Epilog Zing 24
- **Bed Size**: 610×305mm (24″×12″)
- **Power**: 40W CO2 laser
- **Materials**: Wood, acrylic, cardboard, fabric, leather
- **Max Thickness**: 13mm
- **Best for**: Small to medium projects, detailed engraving

### Universal Laser Systems VLS6.60
- **Bed Size**: 1219×610mm (48″×24″)
- **Power**: 60W CO2 laser
- **Materials**: Wood, acrylic, metal (engraving only)
- **Max Thickness**: 19mm
- **Best for**: Large projects, production runs

## Supported Materials

### Safe Materials ✅
- **Wood**: Plywood, MDF, hardwoods, bamboo
- **Acrylic**: Cast acrylic only (not extruded)
- **Paper/Cardboard**: All types
- **Fabric**: Cotton, wool, felt, leather
- **Food**: Wood for cutting boards (food-safe finish required)

### Prohibited Materials ❌
- **PVC**: Produces toxic chlorine gas
- **Polycarbonate**: Produces toxic gases
- **ABS Plastic**: Melts poorly
- **Fiberglass**: Contains dangerous fibers
- **Any material containing chlorine**

## Design Guidelines

### File Formats
- **Vector files**: AI, PDF, SVG for cutting
- **Raster files**: PNG, JPG for engraving
- **Resolution**: 300+ DPI for engraving

### Design Tips
- Use **red lines** (RGB 255,0,0) for cutting
- Use **black fills** for engraving
- Keep cut lines **hairline thickness** (0.001″)
- Leave **3mm minimum** between cut lines
- Design for **material kerf** (~0.1-0.2mm)

## Operation Procedure

1. **Reserve machine time** in advance
2. **Prepare your design file** according to guidelines
3. **Set up material** on the cutting bed
4. **Focus the laser** to material surface
5. **Run test cuts** on scrap material
6. **Execute your cut** with supervision
7. **Clean up** material scraps and debris

## Safety Requirements

- Completed laser safety training
- Constant supervision during operation
- Fire extinguisher within reach
- No flammable materials nearby
- Proper ventilation system running
